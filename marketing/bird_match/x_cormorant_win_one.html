Cormorant Wins Tanager

Out Glory-ed

This is a match one would see in a taboo bazzar who pairs their nightly line up with odd circus-like oppenents, it hard to imagine these two players are both from the same organism class, Aves. One looks like a serpentine dinosaur, and the other, a christmas tree ornament with a lightbulb inside. The Tanager possibly has the most vivid color of any living thing. Even in full rocky mountain daylight, a glimmer will sparkle from a tree as if a shiney mirror is perched, instead you will be delighted to find a fluttering, busy lifeform.

The less likely to win a beutey contest, the Cormorant, on the other, hand has been known to repulse its spectaters at first glance. It has a macaub history for this reason, being persecuted for its goulish impression and kulled almost to extinction in the first part of the last century. From this generational adversity, the Cormorant has barreled onto prominance again. Because of this journey, the underdog take the cake, the Cormorant.
