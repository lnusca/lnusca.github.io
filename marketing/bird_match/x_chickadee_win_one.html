Chickadee Wins Flycatcher

Out Survived

Depending how you feel about talking, we must recognize the social benefits of it for creatures, including us humans. And including the better talker the Chickadee. As the writers of Readers Digest glamorizes many things with its word smithing, none better than saying "bulletins are continually disseminated throughout the company" when refering to their networking skills.

The Flycatcher is more of a one animal show. But a show absolutly. Somersaulting through the air to impress only one other, hopefully its mate. Knowing he is beutiful with nine inch tassel like tail feathers that accent the grace of his flight, he lives a simple life in the bosom of Texas warmth all year. Specialized so well to catch bugs on the wing, he figures he doesn't need anything else, and thats just fine. A home-body is nothing to be embarrased about, especially when defending it sternly, but here the win goes to the well traveled chatter bug, the Chickadee.
